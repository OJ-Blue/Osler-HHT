Epistaxis Grading (IFT)

IFT is a tool to grade the severity of nosebleeds in patients with hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia (Mb. Osler) during the last four weeks. The severity of the nosebleed episodes helps in guiding your treatment and follow-up by your physician.

Helpful Information:

The IFT score ranges from 0 to 30.

  • 0-5: Mild bleeding – This range typically suggests minimal impact on daily life and might not require aggressive treatment.
  • 6-10: Moderate bleeding – You may experience more frequent or bothersome episodes that should be monitored and may require medical attention.
  • 11-15: Severe bleeding – This score indicates frequent and potentially concerning nosebleeds, likely requiring medical intervention and more frequent follow-up.
  • 16-30: Intractable bleeding – This is a high-risk category, suggesting persistent and potentially dangerous nosebleeds. Urgent and ongoing medical treatment is often necessary.

The IFT score helps guide your physician in determining the most appropriate treatment and follow-up strategy for managing your condition.
